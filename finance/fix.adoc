=== The Electronic Trading Ecosystem & Market Roles

Many people would attribute FIX to simply being a messaging standard:

- Tags
- Fields
- Sessions

A vague idea of sending orders and receiving executions.

But to truly understand, we need to change perspective a bit:

**This is not just a protocol on the wire**, it is the wiring diagram of the trading
business itself.

Based on:

. Who are you on the market?
. What is your role?
. What are your obligations?
. What is your risk appetite?

This will proportionately dictate actions such as:

- Whom you connect to
- The direction the information flows
- How many FIX sessions you need
- What each session is allowed to do

[IMPORTANT]
=====
If you know who are the main actors in the electronic trading ecosystem. You can
then easily make the connection as to how their business relationships connect to
FIX connectivity patterns.
=====

_If you understand how the business relationships map to FIX connectivity patterns,
then you will easily understand many of the infrastructure decisions in real FIX
applications, these design decisions will no longer feel 'arbitrary' but with INTENT_

==== Four Core Role Families

Most electronic trading ecosystems can be put into 4 main buckets:

- **Buy side:** Investors and propietary traders who ultimately decide what risk
to take
- **Sell side:** Brokers-dealers and prime brokers who intermediate between clients
and markets
- **Execution venues:** The "places of execution" where orders actually match and
_become trades_
- **Service providers:** Firms that support trading (data, software, connectivity,
clearing, etc), but typically do not trade on their own account with you via
FIX

[IMPORTANT]
====
These labels are entirely legal and regulatory roles, **not technologies**. Some
organizations can wear multiple hats, (a big bank that is both sell-side broker and
a venue operator)
====

There is a specific way that these 4 families connect.

The business imperatives can be related back to concrete FIX session patterns

Buy-Side (Asset Managers, Hedge Funds) - Risk Decisions, Portfolio Management
=> [Fix order flow] => Sell-Side (Broker-Dealers, Primes) - Client access, risk
checks => [FIX executions] => Execution Venues (Exchanges, ATS/MTF) - Order matching,
trade prints

Sell-Side ---> [Drop copy/fills?]

Execution Venues ---> [Drop cop / trade feed]

Buy-Side ==> [Routing Analytics] ==> Service Providers (OMS/EMS, Market Data, Networks,
Clearing) - Tools, plumbing, post-trade

Sell-Side ==> [Clearing, reporting] ==> Service Providers

=== Buy-Side: The Origin of Investment Intent

**Who is the buy-side?**

On the buy-side we find:

Asset managers:: Mutual funds, pension funds, insurance portfolios
Hedge funds:: Often more active, using leverage and derivatives
Proprietary trading firms:: Trading their own capital, sometimes very high-frequency
Wealth managers and family offices:: Managing money for individuals or families

What unites them is that they represent (or are) the ultimate owners of risk. Their
job is to decide _what_ to own or short, in what quantities, and on what time
horizon.

**Commercial incentives and constraints**

Key incentives and constraints for buy-side participants include:

Best execution:: Achieving good prices and low trading costs for their investors,
often measured relative to benchmarks or volume-weighted average prices.
Minimal operational risk:: Avoiding fat-finger errors, missing fills, or misbooked
trades that could damage investor trust
Confidentiality:: Protecting trading intentions from leaking to the broader market
Regulatory obligations:: Complying with reporting rules, investor disclosure, and
sometimes explicit best-executions regulation

All these factors influence how they use FIX. Most buy-side firms do not connect
directly to every exchange. Instead, they rely on sell-side brokers and service
providers.

**Typical Workflows**

From the buy-side perspective, a trading day often looks like this:

- A portfolio manager decides to buy or sell a basket of instruments
- Orders are entered into an **Order Management System (OMS)** or an **Execution
Management System (EMS)** 
- The OMS/EMS routes child orders to one or more brokers via **FIX order-entry
sessions**
- Execution reports, fills, and status updates flow back on the same or closely
related FIX sessions
- At the end of the day, the buy-side reconciles positions and cash using its own
records plus **drop copies** and end-of-day reports from brokers and custodians


== Own research and thoughts

So, imagine you want to pipe into a Broker directly. To get execution reports,
because we want to get trade order data, to book it somewhere else.

- The protocol itself **HAS NO BROADCAST MECHANISM**. FIX Sessions are stateful.

A sesion tracks:

- Sequence numbers
- Resend requests
- Gap fills
- Recovery state
- Heartbeats

If 5 consumers independently attached to one execution stream: **each would need
independent sequencing/recovery semantics**

That naturally becomes:

- Separate FIX sessions
- Or an internal pub/sub layer

Which is exactly what firms implement

**Most Common Industry Solution**

- External connectivity: FIX sessions
- Internal distribution: Message buses/event streams
- Regulatory/risk copies: Drop copy FIX sessions

So, if you really want Execution reports from a trader's perspective to sent
into your system that can be a FIX client receiver: **Ask the broker for a separate
drop copy service/session**. That is typically part of the broker's **rules of engagement**
or service offering, and FIX's own guidance treats drop copy / secondary data distribution
as a distinct use case rather than ordinary order routing.

The idea is for the server to send a message back to a **specific counterparty/session**.
The idea is to write logic that handles a request of getting an execution report that
was executed for a specific order.
That would then get routed to chosen FIX session (your listening session)

The code should decide which session gets which report. If an application duplicates
an execution into multiple sessions, that is an application design choice. Drop copy
is the standard way to do that cleanly and explicitly.
